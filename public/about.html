BearRescue - Technical Review

The Team

  • Alireza Shahnazari, Electrical Engineering, Los Angeles Harbor College
  • Huu Nhan Nguyen, Computer Science, De Anza College
  • Mario Flores-Martinez, Computer Science, Los Angeles Trade Tech

Design

The inspiration behind the CAD design was to make this amazing gauntlet a lifesaver, there were many iterations, but the main goal was to make it so that it would have the most capabilities in practical applications and, at the same time, not get in the way when performing tasks. Also, one of the important functionalities that were incorporated in the gauntlet was its ability to be fully disconnected from the body of the gauntlet and reattached with little to zero steps to a new gauntlet in the case of severe damage to the gauntlet in the field.

Also, after we saw the field of play our flexible design allowed us to use it in my left hand side instead because one of the stops had a wall on the left which made it difficult to properly align our sensors.

Circuit

The process to make the circuit work was a little messy so we desided to use a test bench with half of our parts available so that we were free to move things around however it was convenient to make the sensors work well with the code. We then replicated our design with much clearer chassis to better adapt the amount of parts we used. In the finals ours as we got much more confortable with our circuitry we decided to add a screen for real time readouts as well as the led ring to aid detecting faulty distances.

Coding

Coding the full array of sensors was an oddesey. I definitely benefited from having taken a lot of my free time to tinker with the circuits we were provided in the basic training. It allowed me to iterate faster through code and refactor messy snippets. Due to time constraints I was unable to make the code fully adhere to OOP principles, but I used composition for many components to make the code more flexible and readable.

Additionally, our remote teammate as a CS major benefited greatly from learning how to use GitHub and make pull requests. Also, he learned how to effectively work concurrently on the same project to make marging conflicts less likely.

Finally, in the website design our remote teammate and I were able to relax a little bit from the cruch of learning circuitry on the move as CS majors. One other thing that gave our team a good edge was the use of VS Code instead of the Arduino IDE because the intellisense was pretty helpful for navigating all the methods and properties of the libraries we used for the Hackathon.
